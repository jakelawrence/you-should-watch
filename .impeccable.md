# .impeccable.md — You Should Watch

## Design Context

### Users
Film lovers, typically ages 20–50, with artsy yet refined taste. They come to the app with a specific job: find a good movie to watch tonight, quickly and confidently, without feeling overwhelmed by choice. They are curious and engaged with cinema — not casual scrollers.

### Brand Personality
Useful, inviting, and quietly confident. The experience should feel like a knowledgeable friend recommending a film — calm, considered, never pushy. Three words: **curious, calm, editorial**.

### Aesthetic Direction
The design is moving away from its earlier bold/graphic phase (heavy black borders, loud scenario colors, brutalist UI elements) toward something more refined and minimal — closer in spirit to A24's website aesthetic.

**Reference:** A24's site. Key qualities to draw from:
- Editorial confidence: let typography and imagery carry weight, not decorative borders
- Generous whitespace as a design element, not emptiness
- Restraint with color — let movie poster art be the color; UI stays near-neutral
- Clean typographic hierarchy with strong contrast between display and body
- Minimal UI chrome; every element earns its place

**Anti-references:** Avoid neon, busy layouts, heavy borders used decoratively, Tailwind pastel rainbow cards, or anything that feels like a generic web app.

**Themes:** Light mode (warm cream palette) and dark mode (planned feature). The warm-neutral palette — `#f6f1ea` background, `#1f1b17` near-black, muted slate blue `#4f6f8a` — translates naturally to both.

### Typography System
- **Display / Hero:** Syne (black, heavy) — large, confident headings
- **Section headers:** Special Gothic Expanded One — uppercase, wide, graphic
- **Body / UI:** Raleway 300/400 — elegant geometric with Art Deco inflections, refined without being fragile
- **Principle:** High contrast between display and body weights. Never add weight to body copy; let the display type do the heavy lifting.

### Design Principles
1. **Earn every element.** If it doesn't add clarity or beauty, remove it. Whitespace is not waste — it is the design.
2. **Typography over decoration.** Hierarchy through scale and weight, not borders, backgrounds, or badges. The type system is the design system.
3. **Let posters speak.** Movie art is inherently rich. UI should be near-neutral so poster imagery can breathe and be the focal point.
4. **Refine, don't bold.** The design direction is toward less — thinner borders, quieter colors, more air. When in doubt, reduce rather than add.
5. **Dual-mode ready.** Warm light (cream/near-black) and warm dark (charcoal/near-white) should feel like two expressions of the same identity, not separate themes.
