## Design Context

### Users
Film lovers, typically ages 20–50, with artsy yet refined taste. The job is to find a good movie to watch tonight, quickly and confidently, without feeling overwhelmed by choice.

### Brand Personality
Useful, inviting, and quietly confident. Calm, considered, never pushy. Three words: **curious, calm, editorial**.

### Aesthetic Direction
Moving toward a refined, minimal, editorial aesthetic — closer in spirit to A24's website. Away from heavy black borders, loud scenario colors, and bold/graphic UI elements.

- Let typography and imagery carry weight, not decorative borders
- Generous whitespace as a design element
- Restraint with color — movie poster art is the color; UI stays near-neutral
- Minimal UI chrome; every element earns its place
- Dual-mode: warm light (cream/near-black) and warm dark (planned)

### Design Principles
- **Earn every element.** Whitespace is not waste — it is the design.
- **Typography over decoration.** Hierarchy through scale and weight, not borders or backgrounds.
- **Let posters speak.** UI should be near-neutral so poster imagery can breathe.
- **Refine, don't bold.** Direction is toward less — thinner borders, quieter colors, more air.
- **Dual-mode ready.** Light and dark should feel like two expressions of the same identity.
